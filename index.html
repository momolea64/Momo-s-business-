Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our business acquisition process and services.

What is the typical timeline for a business acquisition?

The acquisition process typically takes 3-6 months from initial contact to closing. This includes due diligence (4-8 weeks), financing approval (2-4 weeks), and legal documentation (2-3 weeks). Complex transactions may take longer depending on business size and structure.

What financing options are available for buyers?

We work with multiple financing partners including SBA loans, traditional bank financing, seller financing, and private equity. Most buyers use a combination of personal investment (10-30%) and financing. Our team helps structure the optimal financing package for your situation.

How is the business valuation determined?

Business valuation is based on multiple factors including financial performance, market conditions, industry multiples, asset values, and growth potential. We use certified business appraisers and multiple valuation methods to ensure fair market pricing.

What support is provided during the transition period?

We provide comprehensive transition support including employee communication, customer retention strategies, operational handover, and ongoing consultation for 90 days post-closing. The current owner typically provides training and transition assistance as part of the agreement.

Are there any hidden fees or additional costs?

Our fee structure is transparent and disclosed upfront. Standard costs include broker commission (paid at closing), legal fees, accounting fees, and due diligence expenses. We provide a detailed cost breakdown during our initial consultation with no hidden surprises.

What happens if the deal doesn't close?

If a transaction doesn't proceed to closing, you're only responsible for any third-party costs incurred (legal, accounting, inspections). Our success fee is only paid upon successful completion. We work diligently to identify and resolve potential issues early in the process.
